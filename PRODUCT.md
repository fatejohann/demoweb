# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Dual audience: (1) Johan's potential business clients in El Salvador who visit his portfolio to evaluate whether to hire him — DermaSkin is the demo that makes them say "I want this for my business"; (2) Fictional patients of the DermaSkin clinic who represent the end-users the demo was designed to serve. Both audiences must be convinced.

The patient persona: adults in San Salvador, 25–55, with disposable income for aesthetic or medical dermatology. Many will be women. They arrive via Google, WhatsApp recommendation, or Instagram. They're evaluating trust before ever walking through the door.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio demo landing page by Johan López demonstrating full-stack design and development skill for clinic-type businesses in El Salvador. DermaSkin is a fictional dermatology clinic in San Salvador with real services, real copy, and a real contact flow — the demo should be indistinguishable from a production site.

Success: a business owner in San Salvador looks at this and immediately wants Johan to build their site.

## Brand Personality

Profesional, elegante, efectiva. The clinic is competent and thorough — not a spa, not a factory. Warm enough that patients feel seen and cared for; precise enough that they trust the doctor's expertise. The visual voice is calm confidence, not cold authority.

## Anti-references

- Generic healthcare blue: the predictable clinical blue + white palette that reads as "hospital", "pharmacy", or "insurance company". Seen it before, trusted it less because of that.
- Over-produced beauty industry: excessive softness, millennial pink, lifestyle photography, wellness-brand gradients. This is a medical practice, not a face serum brand.
- Corporate SaaS patterns: feature card grids, gradient hero CTAs, startup-flavored UI. Wrong register entirely.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth within competence** — every visual choice should pass this test: does it feel human and approachable without losing the authority of a board-certified specialist?
2. **Show portfolio craft, not just clinic services** — this is also Johan's best work; the typography, spacing, and component quality must make developers and business owners equally impressed.
3. **People before procedures** — Dr. Morales and real patient outcomes should feel more prominent than the services list. Trust is built through people.
4. **El Salvador, not a US template** — copy, references, and color warmth should feel native to Central America. Not cold-Northern-European minimalism.
5. **Invisible accessibility** — WCAG 2.1 AA compliance and reduced motion support should feel built-in, not bolted on. Best-in-class is the bar.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as minimum. Full keyboard navigation. Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Adequate contrast on all text including muted variants. Screen reader labels on decorative images and icon-only UI.
